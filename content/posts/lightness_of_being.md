I wonder what the world has in store for us. <!--more-->
![On the Lightness of Being](/images/hqdefault.jpg)


Will we truly become all that we ought to be, or will we only become what we need to be? Will we ever witness alien life? Will there ever be a time without war? And closer to home, will everything be okay on my end?  

Someone once told me, *you have always been, and that is enough.* I’ve carried those words with me, turning them over in my mind, wondering if they hold the weight of truth.  

Every person, rich in their years, bows to wisdom—poignant, distilled, short and sweet. Like aged whisky, refined over time, shaped by fire, honed by years of existence. And yet, even in their wisdom, they say, *I’m not done yet.* In a world that constantly shifts beneath our feet, is there such a thing as being true to oneself? Or are we always becoming, always adapting, always unraveling new versions of who we are?  

I think about death often, not in fear, but in curiosity. When will she visit, or has she always been by my side? I have been close to her a few times, standing at the edge, peering into the unknown. But here I remain. Was it then that she chose to visit, or has she always walked beside us, a quiet observer, waiting?  

I can't wait to see the man I grow into being. What kind of love is in store for me, like an inheritance waiting to be discovered? What trials and tribulations am I meant to overcome? What scars will I inherit? We need to dance, sing, and watch life go on, raging against the abyss of the void around us. We need to stand in a trance-like state, watching the comings and goings of all that was, is, and will be.  

Transitioning from a young man to a man now nearing 25—to me, an age that once felt distant—I fear. I fear. And I fear.  

To you in the future, I have written a thousand times, and here I write for the world to see. **I love you, I’m proud of you, and I can’t wait to meet you—to live through you as I grow into you.**  

I do not claim to be much. But what I am is myself, and I think that is enough.
